Choosing between the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba (Ref. H82505140) and the Longines HydroConquest (Ref. L3.782.4.96.6) is more than just a spec-sheet battle; it is a choice between two different tiers of Swiss watchmaking.

Both watches come from the Swatch Group, ensuring a level of build quality and movement reliability that is hard to beat. However, while the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba is often celebrated as the king of the sub-$1,000 divers, the Longines HydroConquest enters the "entry-level luxury" territory, commanding a higher price for more refined finishes.

In this deep-dive comparison, we explore whether the HydroConquest’s premium price tag is justified or if the Khaki Navy Scuba provides all the "tool watch" you’ll ever need.

### **The Verdict: Which One Should You Buy?**

**The Case for the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba:**  
If your budget is strictly under $1,000, the Hamilton is the undisputed winner. It offers a rugged, military-inspired aesthetic that the Longines lacks. With an 80-hour power reserve and a 300m depth rating, it is a high-performance tool that feels like it can handle a literal battlefield. It is the better choice for the "one-watch collection" buyer who wants something sporty but understated.

**The Case for the Longines HydroConquest:**  
If you value brand prestige and "wrist presence," the Longines is the clear step up. It is significantly thinner (11.9mm vs 12.5mm), making it far more comfortable under a suit cuff. The sunray dial and polished ceramic bezel give it a "jewelry-like" shimmer that the matte Hamilton cannot replicate. It’s for the buyer who wants a luxury diver that looks like a Rolex Submariner competitor without the $10,000 price tag.

## Dial Design and Legibility: Functional vs. Flashy

### Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba: Military DNA

The Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba (H82505140) features a gradient blue dial that transitions from a bright center to a dark navy perimeter. The standout feature is the 24-hour military time scale printed on the inner ring—a signature of the "Khaki" line. The hands are broad "sword" style, providing massive amounts of Super-LumiNova for low-light visibility. The red-tipped second hand adds a tactical vibe, reinforcing that this is a tool first and a fashion accessory second.

### Longines HydroConquest: Luxury Polish

The sunray blue dial on the Longines gives an interesting depth to the watch. The silver-tone secondary color plays well with the stainless steel case and bracelet. Round shapes are used for the hour indices, however, unlike Hamilton, Longines is using large Arab numerals for the 6, 9, and 12 o’clock positions, making these the center of our attention. The date window is located at 3 o’clock to visually balance the dial.

It’s interesting to point out that Longines decided to use a diamond hour hand design (which is somewhat similar to the snowflake hour hand, Tudor’s trademark option for their sports watches). Additionally, the pencil minute hand provides a lot of angles, counterbalancing the large Arab numerals curves. The lollipop second hand connects these elements.

## Bezel

### Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba

The unidirectional bezel on the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba exhibits a classic design commonly seen on dive watches, known as the count-up bezel. This bezel is engraved with a 60-minute scale, traditionally utilized by divers to track their time underwater.

### Longines HydroConquest

The Longines HydroConquest’s bezel is very similar to the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba: uni-directional count-up ceramic bezel, with a 60-minute scale engraved. The Arab numerals on the bezel are slightly bigger - wider to be exact - to better match the numbers on the dial.

## Case: The Thickness Factor

### Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba

The brushed stainless steel case of the Khaki Navy Scuba has a case width of 43 mm, a lug-to-lug distance of 52 mm, and a lug width of 22 mm. The thickness of the watch is 12.53 mm. It offers an impressive water resistance of up to 300 meters (30 bars), which is supported by the screw-down crown.

### Longines HydroConquest

Longines HydroConquest has the same dimensions as the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba. A small difference is the 21 mm lug width (Hamilton is 22 mm), and the Longines is a thinner case overall, measuring at 11.9 mm as opposed to Hamilton’s 12.53 mm. Longines achieved this by using the L888 movement, which is thinner than the Hamilton's H-10. On the wrist, the Longines feels sleek and sophisticated.

**Pro Tip:** Look at the Crown Guards. The Longines features very large, angular, and aggressive crown guards that define its silhouette. The Hamilton has smaller, integrated guards that give the watch a more classic, rounded shape.

## Movement: H-10 vs L888

Both brands are owned by the Swatch Group, meaning they get access to the best ETA-based movements. However, they are tuned differently:

- **Hamilton H-10:** This movement is a "slow-beater," running at 21,600 vibrations per hour (vph). By slowing down the beat rate, Hamilton achieves a massive 80-hour power reserve. It features a Nivachron balance spring, which is an alloy designed to resist magnetic fields from your electronics.
- **Longines L888:** This is a higher-spec movement. It beats at a unique 25,200 vph. This is a "middle ground" that allows for a smoother sweeping second hand than the Hamilton while still maintaining a healthy 72-hour power reserve. Most importantly, the HydroConquest uses a Silicon balance spring. Silicon is even more anti-magnetic and temperature-resistant than Nivachron, marking the Longines as the superior mechanical engine.

## Stainless Steel Bracelet

### Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba

The bracelet of the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba is crafted from brushed stainless steel and adopts the iconic oyster bracelet design, lending it a timeless appeal associated with dive watches. This bracelet design has more of a sporty appearance.

### Longines HydroConquest

The Longines HydroConquest also comes with a stainless steel oyster bracelet, however, it uses a combination of brushed and satin finishes. While an all brushed stainless steel bracelet is very sporty, Longines have the center links with a satin finish that provides an interesting touch of elegance.

## Brand Heritage

In the world of horology, Longines sits a step above Hamilton in the brand hierarchy. Longines is one of the oldest registered watch brands in the world and has a massive global reputation for "Elegance."  
  
Hamilton, conversely, is the "insider's" choice. It’s the brand of Hollywood (featured in Interstellar and Tenet) and American military history. While it doesn't have the "luxury" tag of Longines, it has a "cool factor" that many enthusiasts prefer.

## Summary

Overall, the Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba and the Longines HydroConquest are very similar with a few small differences. They’re both 43 mm wide, however, the crown guards have different designs: Hamilton has smoother curves, while Longines is using angles for a more robust appearance.

The Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba doesn’t have a date complication, while the Longines HydroConquest’s date window is at the 3 o’clock position.

The movements provide similar power reserves: Hamilton’s 80 hours, and the Longines’ 72 hours will be equally comfortable for everyday life. What we’re missing from both is the exhibition case back.

The oyster bracelet has the same butterfly clasp, however the design of the links is different as Hamilton has an all brushed stainless steel bracelet, while Longines is using a brushed and polish finish combination.

## Verdict: Hamilton Khaki Navy Scuba vs Longines HydroConquest

Choose the Hamilton if:

- Budget is a deciding factor, as the Hamilton comes in below $1,000.
- You’re looking for a watch that can be dressed up or down easily.

Choose the Longines HydroConquest if:

- Brand power and heritage are important factors. Longines has high-end, entry-level luxury timepieces, and having a timepiece from a brand like that could be a factor.
- If you already have a more elegant watch you can wear for more formal occasions. The Longines HydroConquest definitely has a sporty appearance.
