The Morgan watch, a distinguished timepiece named after Sir Morien Bedford Morgan, the visionary behind Concorde—Britain's pioneering supersonic passenger aircraft, stands as a testament to both heritage and innovation. A key feature of this watch is its Faraday soft-iron inner cage, a design element that neutralizes harmful magnetic fields around the movement during flight. While this feature may seem antiquated in the context of daily life, it proves surprisingly relevant in today's technology-driven world, shielding the delicate movement from the constant magnetic interference emitted by ubiquitous devices like phones and laptops.
