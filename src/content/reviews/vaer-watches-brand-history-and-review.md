## The History of VAER

VAER was founded in Venice, California in 2016 by Ryan Torres and Reagan Cook. Their aim was to create affordable watches that are perfect as everyday go-to timepieces due to their durability. Admittedly, the founders knew they were signing up for an uphill battle. 2016 was the height of fast fashion, a world dominated by lightning-speed trends driven by digital connectivity. Nevertheless, the idea was born and a growing sense of possibility pervaded. They knew there was still a place for well-made analog watches, and there always will be. We could not agree more!

So, in an attempt to create a legacy of lasting quality and design, VAER was born. 

Before VAER watches became available to the public, Ryan Torres and Reagan Cook tested the first prototypes with real life situations: these watches experienced free diving, surfing, drop tests, boiling water submersions, even a high altitude exposure in the Himalayas. The takeaway was that the crown was the weakest link, and measures were taken to fortify the impermeability of the timepieces. As a result, VAER watches have locking screw-down crowns —a feature we usually see on high-end and luxury dive watches.

### An Independent Brand Committed to Quality

The growth of VAER watches was not rushed, and they slowly gained the trust and support from watch enthusiasts and outdoorsmen. Their growth was supported by customer loyalty, and it played a critical part in avoiding the influence of external funding. We really like the fact that VAER’s sustainable-growth business model prioritizes product value over profit margins.

## Where Are VAER Watches Made?

After their first year in business, VAER moved their watch assembly operation to the United States. While it caused some logistical challenges —since a US watch manufacturing network was non-existent —VAER still took the risk. The hard work paid off: in the spring of 2018, the first batch of American assembled watches were successfully released, providing customers with the most (by far) affordable USA-assembled watches on the market.

## VAER Watch Movements

VAER is using a variety of movements for their different models. The most affordable pieces will have high quality quartz movements, while solar-powered watches are also available. There are several models with automatic movement, they are usually Miyota movements (most frequently used calibers are the Miyota 9039, and Miyota 9015). Both of these movements are 3-hand movements providing a 40-hour power reserve, the difference between them is whether the date functionality is available or not. Miyota is a very trusted and reliable Japanese automatic movement owned by the Citizen Group.

## Takeaway: Are VAER Watches Good?

VAER makes great watches in our opinion. The timepieces are durable and they go through rigorous testing to make sure they’re long-lasting. VAER uses reliable movements for all quartz, solar-powered, and automatic variations. Last but certainly not least, while still a new brand, they already had a significant impact on American watchmaking, and we wholeheartedly support their efforts!
