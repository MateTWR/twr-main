Nivada Grenchen has a rich history that dates back to the early 20th century, however, the brand made a comeback recently with some exciting reissues and new designs. The timing of the revival of Nivada Grenchen was impeccable: we’re in 2018, and the popularity of vintage designs, smaller case sizes was on the rise when new owners Guillaume Laidet and Remi Chabrat purchases the licensing use of the Nivada Grenchen name, and its back-catalog of successful models no longer produced.

Let’s take a deeper look at the fascinating journey of Nivada Grenchen, exploring its popular collections, and watch designs that contributed to the very successful comeback of the brand.

## The History of Nivada Grenchen

Nivada Grenchen was founded in 1926 in Grenchen, Switzerland. At the beginning, the brand’s focus was on elegant everyday watches that can withstand the rigors of day to day use.

Nivada Grenchen became synonymous with commitment to quality and durability.

### The Different Names of Nivada Grenchen

In 1939, Nivada partnered with the New York-based Croton Watch Company for distribution in the United States. This move was necessary as Movado had a successful legal challenge against Nivada's use of its name in the US market, claiming it sounded too similar to Movado. In my personal opinion, this sounds like a stretch, and I’m actually surprised about Movado being successful in this legal battle.

As a result, watches that were marketed as Nivada or Nivada Grenchen in Europe were sold under the Croton brand name in the US.

As you can imagine, this branding strategy has caused considerable confusion among collectors regarding the models. There were watches labeled in all combinations you can think of: Nivada, Nivada Grenchen, Croton, Croton Nivada, and Croton Nivada Grenchen.

The 1960s marked the pinnacle of Nivada's production, with the Chronomaster Aviator Sea Diver, launched in 1963, becoming one of the most popular models after the Antarctic dive watch, which was the brand's most popular model in the 1950s.

### Resurgence of Nivada Grenchen

Like many others, Nivada was significantly impacted by the quartz crisis of the 1970s. However, the brand saw a resurgence in 2018 under new ownership, which was the beginning of the comeback of the Chronomaster Aviator Sea Diver. This was followed by the re-release of the Antarctic in 2020, and then the Depthmaster and Datomaster in 2021 and 2022, respectively. These reissued models closely mirror the original designs and functionalities of their historic counterparts.

## Milestones of Nivada Grenchen

Throughout its history, Nivada Grenchen has been at the forefront of innovation in the watch industry. 

In the 1950s, the brand achieved a significant milestone with the introduction of the Antarctic model. This watch was designed for the U.S. Navy's Operation Deep Freeze, a testament to its durability and precision in the most extreme conditions. The Antarctic became a symbol of Nivada Grenchen's capability to produce watches that not only tell time but also withstand the tests of adventure and exploration.

Another notable achievement was the development of the Chronomaster Aviator Sea Diver in the 1960s, a highly versatile chronograph that catered to both aviators and divers. This model showcased Nivada Grenchen's dedication to serving the needs of professionals who demanded accuracy and reliability in challenging environments.

## Movements Used by Nivada Grenchen

Nivada Grenchen's watches are equipped with different movement types, and calibers. All of them are excellent quality with great reliability. 

They have timepieces with quartz and mecaquartz movements (like the Seiko VK63A caliber), manual hand-winding (Valjoux 23 caliber), and ETA-based automatic movements.

## Takeaway: Are Nivada Grenchen Watches Good Watches?

Nivada Grenchen's brand journey is a story of how a small Swiss brand evolved into a global watchmaking icon. Resilience, and unwavering commitment to quality. Nivada Grenchen represents the pinnacle of Swiss watchmaking, with a legacy that spans nearly a century.

I think it’s great to see the revival of the brand. The 1970s was difficult for a lot of brands, and Nivada Grenchen was no exception. Luckily, their original designs are available again, and new collections are a very respectful nod towards the brand’s rich history.

Whether it's the rugged Antarctic model or the elegant Chronomaster, Nivada Grenchen watches are designed to be not just timekeepers, but companions for life.

In a world where the pace of life continues to accelerate, a Nivada Grenchen watch on the wrist serves as a reminder of the enduring values of quality, reliability, and beauty. 

As the brand moves forward, it continues to draw on its rich heritage while embracing the challenges of the future. For those who appreciate the art of watchmaking, Nivada Grenchen offers a connection to a tradition of excellence that is timeless.
