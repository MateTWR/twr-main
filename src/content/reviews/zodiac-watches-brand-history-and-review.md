## The History of Zodiac Watches

Zodiac was founded in 1882 by Ariste Calame in Le Locle, Switzerland. The company initially focused on producing pocket watches, but they created their first wristwatch in 1895.

The Zodiac name itself was patented in 1908, with  focus on precision—resulting in the launch of one of the first automatic wrist watches in the 1930s.

Later, in 1953, the Zodiac Sea Wolf was introduced, becoming one of the world's first serious diver watches manufactured for the masses.

The Sea Wolf grew in popularity among military and professional divers, establishing Zodiac as a much respected name in the world of dive watches.

### Who Makes Zodiac Watches?

The Quartz Crisis during the 1970s and 1980s affected the Zodiac watch production, as it did for many other Swiss watch brands. Finally, in 1984, the company declared bankruptcy, and while they were acquired twice during the 1990s, the efforts to recover and improve the brand recognition failed. Then, in 2001, Zodiac  was acquired by Fossil, Inc., a global fashion accessories company. Under Fossil’s ownership, Zodiac underwent an explosive and inspiring renaissance, focusing on the brand’s heritage and the reintroduction of popular classic models.

## Are Zodiac Watches Luxury?

While there are no clear definitions for the categories, we classify Zodiac as premium, or high-end watch brand. While Fossil Group, who owns Zodiac, is known for their entry-level fashion brand watches, Zodiac stands as a distinguished beacon of brand legacy, acquired through their longstanding commitment to quality and reliability.

## Zodiac Watch Movements

Most of the Zodiac watches use automatic movements, mainly STP (Swiss Technology Production) movements. STP is owned by the Fossil Group, and they manufacture their automatic movements in Switzerland. The most used calibers are the STP 1-11, and the STP 3-13. These movements are on par with Sellita and ETA movements when it comes to quality.

Some Zodiac watches use Sellita movements, and the quartz models are usually equipped with Ronda movements.

## Zodiac Brand Reputation

Zodiac continues to be recognized for its history of innovation in dive watches and remains a popular choice among watch enthusiasts. The brand frequently flies under the radar, which is probably due to some of their challenges around brand awareness during the 1990s, and early 2000s. 

With the acquisition by Fossil Group, Zodiac certainly reestablished their place in the world of watches as a heritage brand. Staying true to their original designs, they create high quality, reliable timepieces.

## Takeaway: Are Zodiac Watches Good Watches? Are They Worth the Money?

Zodiac watches are indisputably reliably made, high quality timepieces. Both on the outside and in, complete with top quality Swiss movements. Boasting rich history reaching back all the way to the late 1800s / early 1900s, the current models are thoughtfully designed with the original ideas in mind. Zodiac certainly plays a significant role in the  history of watchmaking, and we’re excited to see what the future has in store for the brand.

A decade later, in the early 2010s, Zodiac launched a new series of Sea Wolf watches, which paid homage to the vintage aesthetics of the 1950s originals.
