## The History of Doxa Watches

Founded in 1889 by Georges Ducommun in Le Locle, Switzerland, Doxa quickly established itself as a maker of high-quality timepieces, mainly with dress watches at first.

The founder, Georges Ducommun was 21 years old when he founded Doxa under the name “Georges Ducommun, Fabriques Doxa”.

“Doxa” is the Greek word for glory. The meaning behind the name also represents the brand’s ultimate goal: a brand reputation for value, quality and exceptional workmanship.

Georges Ducommun was passionately committed to creating watches that embodied unyielding durability and exact precision. After his death in 1936, the leadership of the company passed to his son-in-law, Jacques Nardin, who was also the grandson of Ulysse Nardin, the founder of the prestigious watch brand of the same name. Under Jacques' guidance, Doxa maintained its emphasis on producing timepieces suited for travel and sports, while still catering to a diverse clientele. The brand continued to introduce innovative features, such as alarm functions, ring-watches, and technical enhancements like a date-displaying pointer and a jumping second hand.

### Innovation and Growth

A major turning point for Doxa came with the introduction of the Doxa SUB in 1967. This was one of the first dive watches intended for the general public and featured a patented no-decompression dive table bezel, a bright orange dial for better underwater readability, and a robust case design.

### Challenges and Revival

Doxa, like many traditional watchmakers, faced challenges during the quartz crisis in the 1970s. In an attempt to adapt, Doxa joined forces with a group of Swiss watch manufacturers, pooling their resources together in an attempt of being economically more efficient. This strategy ultimately proved unsuccessful. Following a sale, Doxa ceased its operations around 1980.

The Jenny family acquired the Doxa brand in 1997, and started to re-introduce popular models in limited quantities at first.

The revival proved to be a resounding success, and Doxa saw a surge in popularity, especially among diving watch enthusiasts. The brand began reissuing classic models like the Doxa SUB, which garnered a cult following.

## Doxa Watch Movements: What Movement Does Doxa Use?

Doxa watches are usually equipped with Swiss automatic movements. These movements are generally either ETA movements, or Sellita movements.

Since 1793, ETA SA, a Swiss movement manufacturer, has been at the forefront of creating and crafting calibers for the watchmaking industry. This long-standing expertise has been instrumental in establishing ETA SA as one of the largest producers of watches and movements in the world. ETA is part of The Swatch Group.

Sellita, established in 1950 and located in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Neuchâtel, Switzerland, is a renowned producer of mechanical movements for watches. Initially, it played a significant role as a key assembly partner for ETA's movements until 2003. Post-2003, leveraging the expired patents of ETA movements, Sellita began creating its own movements. Today, it stands as a principal movement manufacturer in the Swiss watch industry.

## Doxa Brand Reputation

Doxa continues to innovate while staying true to its brand heritage. The brand has released updated versions of its classic models and introduced new timepieces that reflect its commitment to quality and innovation in watchmaking.

Throughout its history, Doxa has remained a respected name in Swiss watchmaking, known for its commitment to precision, quality, and innovation, especially in the field of diving watches.

## Takeaway: Are Doxa Watches Good Watches? Are They Worth the Money?

All in all, Doxa's reputation is built on its pioneering role in dive watch innovation, distinctive design, commitment to quality, and a strong connection with the diving community. Despite its challenges in the past, the brand has successfully managed to retain and even grow its appeal among watch enthusiasts. The brand is a pillar of watch history, and they’re building on their classic designs, which we naturally love - just look at the SUB 200 collection. Doxa is a great choice for a true watch enthusiast who’s looking for a timepiece from a brand with an impressive heritage. Doxa might have been unfairly overlooked up until a few years ago, but today, they’re becoming more and more popular (again), and rightly so!
